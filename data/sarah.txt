Sarah is a timeless name with deep historical and cultural roots. Often associated with strength and grace, Sarah has been a popular choice for centuries. In the Bible, Sarah was the wife of Abraham and the mother of Isaac, known for her faith and role in the lineage of nations. The name itself means "princess" in Hebrew, symbolizing nobility and leadership. Across different cultures, Sarah has remained a favorite, representing elegance, wisdom, and resilience. Whether in literature, film, or everyday life, Sarahs are often depicted as compassionate, thoughtful, and strong individuals.
